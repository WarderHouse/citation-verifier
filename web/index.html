Citation Verifier

Paste your reference list and check which references actually exist, before you cite them.

Each reference's title and DOI are looked up in two public databases (CrossRef and OpenAlex), from your browser. No private data, no AI, no account.

One reference per line, in whatever style you have. The checker shows the title and DOI it read from each line, so you can see what it looked up.

Found means a database confirmed it (one is enough). Double-check the DOI means the DOI resolves but to a differently-titled work. Likely grey literature means it has no DOI and is not in the scholarly databases, so it is probably a book, report, or website to verify yourself. Not found means the DOI did not resolve anywhere, which may signal a wrong or fabricated reference. Finding a work confirms it exists, not that it says what you claim.
